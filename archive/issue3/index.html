Local Drag Show Interrupted by ‘Impassioned’ Bystander

A drag performance at a local gay bar was interrupted Thursday evening by a man who grabbed the microphone and then refused to give it back, despite active efforts on the part of staff.


20 minutes into the hour long show, an older man identified only as “James Montgomery Martins III” grabbed a microphone and launched into a 15-minute speech, interrupted only briefly by the squawking and scrabbling as the queens chased him around the bar.


The older man, or “James” as we will refer to him here, led a surprisingly nuanced discussion on gender, gender presentation, and how body norms and expectations have caused a wedge both within the queer community and the transgender community on its own. He even took questions from the audience, who seemed confused but excited by the events transpiring.


“He actually made some really good points,” one witness who declined to be identified said. “I really liked the part where he said gender is both a performance and an act of violence. It was pretty funny, since Lezzy Lesbourne hit him with a broom right after.”


While the bar did ask the man to leave, we were informed that he has since been asked to host the following drag show at the bar, but just to “keep it to a tight 5” on the next go-around.

Executive Killed in Shooting Was CEO of Orphan Crusher™

The victim killed in an unexpected Denver shooting on Tuesday was a prominent executive leading up Orphan Crusher™, it has been revealed.

Gregory Kissinger, 52, was killed by a gunman who stormed the building where he worked, Orphan Crusher™ confirmed.

Kissinger was killed in the lobby of the building. He does not appear to have been specifically targeted by the shooter.

When asked about a motive, local police suggested that it may have been a random act of violence erupting from the shooter’s likely mental disturbance. A note found at the scene of the crime read, “I’m so crazy so I’m gonna kill someone, but I actually love the way Orphan Crusher™ has reduced the population of parentless kids in the U.S. Thanks Orphan Crusher™!” The Denver PD says the shooter, a 12 year old who recently lost his parents in a car accident, was killed by police.

Kissinger had been a rising star at Orphan Crusher™ for years, playing a key role in the firm’s $300 billion estimation this past year.

“Words cannot express the devastation we feel that our beloved colleague, Gregory Kissinger, lost his life in a tragic incident yesterday,” Orphan Crusher™ said in a statement. “We are grateful for all he did for the business, and look forward to carrying on his legacy in our work.”

The firm added they are renaming one of their newly launched SuperCrusher™ machines in his honor. “We know it’s what he would have wanted. He loved contributing to a cause greater than himself.”

Although Orphan Crusher™ is among the largest commercial orphan processors in the country, the part of the business Kissinger oversaw was primarily related to coordination with small, family-run orphan crushing businesses nationally.

Vote to form committee to consider student torture passes HCS Board after public outcry

By: Ella Nikitin


HARTLAND, MI — A committee has been formed by the Hartland Consolidated Schools board of education to look into ways to implement ways to improve student behavior, including torture of students.


The resolution to form this committee was proposed by trustee Amber McKinney at a meeting on Monday, June 23rd, which lasted nearly four hours, and had two hours of public comment, as well as several disruptions from members of the public during the meeting.


Student torture as a means to improve student behavior has been a talking point of the board ever since Gwen Rutledge, Patricia Rogers, and Amber McKinney were elected in November of last year, when they ran together, along with Martin Massey, who did not win, on a slate promising to “eliminate disciplinary issues at Hartland Schools”. Their website included talking points such as “using negative sensory input to motivate better behaviors.” However, when accused by community members of supporting torture, Rutledge denied this, saying that “negative sensory input is a common-sense solution to discipline issues and is not a form of torture”


Several members of the public spoke at the meeting’s call to the public. A resident of Brighton, MI spoke and said “Torturing students is wrong and is a violation of the eighth amendment”. However, a few members of the public were in support of the resolution, with one saying it “would resolve the problem of students vaping in the bathrooms”.


When it came time to consider the resolution on the agenda, trustee Gwen Rutledge took the time to explain his thoughts on the resolution, saying, “People keep saying that I’m trying to torture students. However, that’s not actually what is happening today. Instead, we are simply forming a committee to consider torture. To say that we are implementing student torture today is a gross representation of the truth. Any policies this committee comes up with still have to be approved by the full board”.


A few members of the public shouted at the board after Rutledge made this comment, with one yelling “Liar!”. Those members of the public were removed from the meeting by a county sheriff’s deputy that was present at the meeting.


Board treasurer Tara Baker, however took an opportunity to criticize the resolution, saying “It is important for students to be supported and to not feel like they could be subjected to torture when they come to school. That is why I will be voting no on this resolution”.


Board president Joseph Nestor said, “I believe it is important that we can all come together and discuss this issue with civility and professionalism. It is important that both sides can have dialogue and come together on a compromise that makes everyone happy”. However, he said that “we also need to consider that other solutions could address this issue”. He proposed an amendment to the resolution to change the language from “consider student torture and other methods” to “consider student torture or other methods”, which passed 7-0.


The board voted 4-3 to form the committee, with trustees Ray Hagans, Tara Baker, and Chris Conn voting no. After the vote, the board then voted unanimously to appoint trustees Ray Hagans, Joseph Nestor, and Patricia Rogers to the newly formed committee.
