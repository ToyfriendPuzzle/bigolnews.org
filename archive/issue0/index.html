Puppies Starving as Kinksters Turn to Kibble for Their Pleasure

In a story just released by the Associated Press, a new craze called “puppymoding” is sweeping the nation—and the shelves of our grocery stores. When someone goes “puppymode” they crave belly rubs, long walks, and, you guessed it, doggy kibble.

This new trend could have dire consequences. As teens chow down on dog chow, Fido might be missing breakfast, because every major brand is reporting huge upticks in sales. Several stores ran out altogether. We talked to one Petco employee, who agreed to speak under the condition of anonymity. According to him, “basically only the shit tasting brands are left most of the time, my girlfriend won’t even suck me off after a bowl of this slop.”

We decided to see what the fuss was all about, and sent our investigative reporter, Todd Howard, to the scene of the crime. According to Todd, “Please stop calling me Todd, my name is Rufus and I am a good boy.” He then proceeded to chase his newly transplanted tail for 5 minutes before taking a nap.

Ripley’s ‘Dumbest Boy Alive’ Receives First Successful Brain Transplant


Speaking of transplants, one boy described as “so dumb we couldn’t find a good enough metaphor for him, just trust us,” was the lucky recipient of the world’s first successful brain transplant. A modern marvel, many have wondered if it could be done, and now science has the answer—yes. But it may not be as easy to answer ethics questions this surgery raises.


Will he be the same person?


What kinds of changes could this cause in a person?


Is this a slippery slope leading to even more radical changes, like altering a body’s natural hormones or god forbid, changing your name?


While he can’t answer all those questions, the boy who once won Ripley’s Stupidest Man Alive contest, Jason Quigley, had this to say: “I feel kind of like the Ship of Thesis. But a little different. Maybe that’s not a great metaphor.” The poor bastard.
