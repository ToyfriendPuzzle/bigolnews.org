Man Fights Insurance Company Over War Exclusion

By: Montrose Fell

PC: brianneises on Pixabay




Octavius (52) of Troy, IL has filed a lawsuit with his insurance broker, the Glooby Company, which acts as intermediary for Divided Healthcare insurance, after his medical claim was denied for injuries sustained in a conflict with nearby Rome, IL. Glooby claims that the dispute, which involved a giant wooden horse, constituted an act of war and therefore is excluded under exclusion 571 on page 1198 of Octavius's employer-provided healthcare policy. Octavius's lawyers argue that the horse-based conflict was instead an act of terrorism and therefore would be included according to clause 889. While the litigation is ongoing, Octavius has flown to Turkey for treatment and a hair transplant.

Local Cop Saves Teen from Fentanyl Poisoning,

“I Was Just Doing My Job”

PC: aitoff on Pixabay



This past Thursday, a local teen bit off more than he could chew, when he ate what he thought was a marijuana edible. But what was actually in the edible was significant amounts of the much more dangerous drug, fentanyl. Within minutes, the 16 year old had passed out in the local park and his friends contacted authorities.


Local police, like Officer Dan Hinkle, have been trying to undercut the illicit drug market in the area for years. In an effort to diminish sales, they flooded the local market with their own concoctions–edibles that look like standard weed gummies, but are actually laced with fentanyl. According to Hinkle, “these bad boys can put you on your ass for like 24-48 hours if you’re not careful.” Hinkle was the officer who responded to the scene, where the teen was unresponsive. He called up the undercover unit, who then identified the teen as a recent client. This is when Hinkle leapt into action. He administered Narcan, a medication used to reverse the effects of opioids, and as soon as the teen woke up, he cuffed him and took him down to the station.


“This is such a horrific example of what the drug industry is doing to our youth. But today? I was just doing my job. I don’t even feel like a hero. I just feel so grateful that I get to help the community every day.”


There has been a nearly 40X increase in overdose deaths in the area since this new drug enforcement program has started, further evidence that these interventions are needed right now more than ever.

Lincoln and Grimsby Schoolchildren Found Consuming Human Bones - Autopsy Impossible

PC: andykahle1 on pixabay


After reports of several missing local pets near schools in Lincoln and Grimsby, officials finally decided to check the school grounds—and they were shocked by what they found. 46 partial skeletons were found scattered across multiple elementary and middle school campuses, 12 of which were identifiably human. Of the remains found, only one still had some meat on its bones. Most of these remains were charred and riddled with gnaw marks, and many of the bones had been cracked open so the marrow could be sucked out. One police officer vomited on finding what has been termed “the cadaver stacks.”


85 local children have been implicated in the murder and consumption of the as-of-yet unidentified remains. Police are dumbfounded as to how to move forward, given the huge number of suspects and the lack of anyone who truly gives a toot about any of the potential victims.


“Maybe we just chalk this up to kids being kids?” one officer suggested at the press conference this morning. Police are currently declining to press charges.

Local Pizzeria and Perfume Shop Cited by City for Being “A Stink-Infested Wasteland”

By: Tanya Gerber (like the baby)

PC: JillWellington on Pixabay



Skunky Mike's Pizzeria and Perfume Shop is a historic landmark in downtown Silver Sprung, Maryland, known for giving you a slice of pie and a good sniff while you’re at it. But as new community members like me have moved into the area, the intense cheesy, breezy, and floral scents have started to cause quite a stink. Their new perfume, which hoped to be a signature scent for the company, was pumped through the vents of all local businesses for 30 minutes last week, in what my neighbor, Erikah, described as “basically chemical warfare".


The reviews are in, and let’s just say, they aren’t looking very good.

These reviews from paying customers who willingly purchased the perfume can only begin to demonstrate the intensity, potency, and disgust that arrived when this scent was pumped through the air conditioning systems of 32 downtown businesses, including my hardware store. What followed feels like the adult version of a kids Chuck E Cheese pizza party—vomiting, crying, and full-on tantrums ensued as the stink permeated the air. Several cars crashed as people rushed to evacuate, assuming a gas leak had occurred.


Silver Sprung city officials cited Skunky’s for a new infraction simply titled “Why Would Anyone Do This?” accompanied by a $500 penalty. When I asked for comment, Silver Sprung mayor Janice Ngibi simply said, “If you are going to create a stink-infested wasteland in our downtown, you better be prepared to pay the costs. And the cost right now is $500.”

Song of the Worm


By: garbage_girl

PC: idek but it’s here


THE worm, the rich worm, has a noble domain

In the field that is stored with its millions of slain;

The charnel-grounds widen, to me they belong,

With the vaults of the sepulchre, sculptured and strong.

The tower of ages in fragments is laid,

Moss grows on the stones, and I lurk in its shade;

And the hand of the giant and heart of the brave

Must turn weak and submit to the worm and the grave.

Daughters of earth, if I happen to meet

Your bloom-plucking fingers and sod-treading feet--

Oh! turn not away with the shriek of disgust

From the thing you must mate with in darkness and dust.

Your eyes may be flashing in pleasure and pride,

'Neath the crown of a Queen or the wreath of a bride;

Your lips may be fresh and your cheeks may be fair--

Let a few years pass over, and I shall be there.

Cities of splendour, where palace and gate,

Where the marble of strength and the purple of state;

Where the mart and arena, the olive and vine,

Once flourished in glory; oh! are ye not mine?

Go look for famed Carthage, and I shall be found

In the desolate ruin and weed-covered mound;

And the slime of my trailing discovers my home,

'Mid the pillars of Tyre and the temples of Rome.

I am sacredly sheltered and daintily fed

Where the velvet bedecks, and the white lawn is spread;

I may feast undisturbed, I may dwell and carouse

On the sweetest of lips and the smoothest of brows.

The voice of the sexton, the chink of the spade,

Sound merrily under the willow's dank shade.

They are carnival notes, and I travel with glee

To learn what the churchyard has given to me.

Oh! the worm, the rich worm, has a noble domain,

For where monarchs are voiceless I revel and reign;

I delve at my ease and regale where I may;

None dispute with the worm in his will or his way.

The high and the bright for my feasting must fall--

Youth, Beauty, and Manhood, I prey on ye all:

The Prince and the peasant, the despot and slave;

All, all must bow down to the worm and the grave.
