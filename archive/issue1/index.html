Local Story Goes Unread (Under Review)

by: Ms. Trivia Cardoon


A cockroach, 6 months old, died today.


It was found stuck between the oven and the fridge in that crack where food always spills and you always say you'll clean it up later (and you will, later), next to a pile of dry noodle shards and mulched broccoli florets.


Experts say its likely the insect ate so much it choked and coughed and sputtered and died.


Experts say cockroaches are born with a natural greed and that is why they crawl into dark crevices where things that don't belong to cockroaches are left behind until you come back to clean them (which you will, later, of course).


Experts say not to feel bad for greedy cockroaches that don't know what they do and don't deserve and eat so much they choke and sputter and die on what doesn't belong to them.


A moth,  a month old, died today.


A moth, two months old, died today.


A moth, one month and one week old, died today.


A moth, three months and eight days old, died today.


A moth, three months and two weeks old, died today.


A moth, two years old, died today.


A moth, a month and twelve days old, died today.


A moth, three years old, died today.


They were found together inbetween the musty old sweaters you say you'll wear sometime this year (sometime this year).


They were found flattened and crushed under the shoes and boots on the shoe rack where they were put without looking, except the ones that were found grey and withered caught in the weave of your old sweaters where they starved and turned to dust instead.


Experts say this is why you've been finding holes in all your favourite shirts lately.

Experts say when moths are born their mothers leave them here, because here is as good as anywhere and here isn't like out there at least and they were left somewhere like here once too and they're sure it will be fine, which it wasn't. And when they're born they eat what's in sight because what they see is all they know and all they see is your favourite shirt so they leave holes in your favourite shirt because that's all they know, and all they can do is what they know.


Experts say moths can grow up in a month if they're very lucky and their mothers leave them all the things they need and they eat what they can see until they're full of it and there's great big holes in your favourite shirts and they're wriggling fat and happy.

And experts say when they grow up they lose their mouths and they leave no more holes; but some are not lucky, or else they don't eat their fill, or they don't see what there is to know, and they take many years to grow, and they have many years to leave holes in your favourite shirts.


But eventually if they don't get flattened and the holes they leave get bigger they lose their mouths too, and they get their wings. And all the moths fly around your closet, bouncing from wall to wall and shirt to sweater and boot to shoe, because all they know is all they see and all they see is your closet. And if they're lucky they can see the crack where you reach inside and pry your way inside to look at those old sweaters and remind yourself to wear them sometime this year (which you will, later) and they fly away through the crack and become mothers in the light and leave their babies somewhere else, dark and full of favourite shirts like this, because that is what they know.


But some don't know about the crack because they never see the crack because, experts say, maybe they just don't look that way because something is wrong with them? And they stay in the dark and flutter around until they get crushed by a careless shoe, or caught in an old sweater's strands, or just spiral and spiral until they can't spiral anymore and they turn to dust; because now they have no mouth and they can't see anything and eating what they saw was all they knew, so now they don't do anything.


Experts say you should get some mothballs soon, or you'll keep finding holes in your favourite shirts.

Local Bear Found with Hand in the Honeypot

by: seeking_bear


Thursday, May 22, one local bear found himself on the wrong side of the law–again. After an FBI raid of a local artisanal grocery store, the 8 foot brown bear was found with his paws in not one, but two open pots of honey–with more than a dozen others scattered around him on the floor.


Known to his friends as “Winnie,” his jovial, if bumbling, nature was noted by several acquaintances we spoke with–even if his proclivity for honey sometimes got in the way.


Winnie was well known for visiting online message boards, making enemies with anyone who suggested that “honey should be left to the bees”. It seems his online activism spurred his later deviancy, as he fell in with a much rougher crowd.


Leaked messages from a former friend,  “FreeBearIssues87,” indicated that Winnie had been traveling a dark path for a while. His first “heist” had involved taking a pot of honey from a neighbor, which he received a slap on the wrist for given his lack of criminal background. The leaked messages indicated that Winnie was rapidly escalating his approach, and that he was one part of a larger group dedicated to guzzling honey in an increasingly “anti-bear era.” The full messages found with his online profile have yet to be released by investigators.


Though he insists he acted alone, local bear organizations have been more active than ever since this incident was reported. The FBI is looking into who his accomplices may have been and what other potential activities could crop up next.

I had found the Hyperbolic Time Chamber available to rent on a really crusty Airbnb knockoff called “budk.com.” I booked a meeting with a guy, whose name was Chuck Lee, and we discussed how I was going to use the hyperbolic time chamber. Chuck was a businessman and was offering a service, so he told me. I got to determine the interior, with different packages for different levels of luxury. So I went with the best package. Crazy expensive, but I think the unfathomable debt I went into for this opportunity will be more than worth it when I have finished reading every book ever written forever. And after I have read every book ever written forever, I’ll probably have a pretty good idea of how the world works and I don’t think I’ll really need to pay off that debt anytime soon.

There were hundreds of ingredients stored in everything from mason jars to capsule company capsules, and I felt like a ruler of old. Op-Eds

Email has become such a painful means of communication in recent years.

Typically, I open my inbox and see primarily advertisements and a few business communications. On some occasions, I receive a passive-aggressive letter from my parents or a random communication from someone I fell out with years ago.


I am frustrated by this because I think there is a lot of merit to long-form communication. Email was supposed to be the easier and less wasteful way of writing letters, but no one uses it that way anymore. I remember even when I was a kid, it was the expectation that I stay in touch with my friends when I went on vacation by writing them emails.


Now, full sentiments and experiences are expected to be conveyed in one-line text messages. People are easily frustrated by "paragraph texters". I think it's not just me whose expectations of email have changed. At a show recently, the crowd started booing when email was mentioned.


I personally would like to shift the balance around email. On my next trip, I think I'd prefer to get a few friends on an email list rather than attempting to convey everything through Instagram stories. I also hope newsletters like these can help people feel like their email is giving them a better view of their community rather than just trying to sell them something or torture them.

AIO Study Stuns Scientists Across The Globe

by: LaylaSaturn

Scientists in the field of macrogenetics from the Amerigan Institute of Organometrics (AIO) published a study in the journal 'Organa' on Wednesday which they claim sheds a new light on the cause of the so called 'Cluster Deviance'.  This 'deviance', which the scientific consensus refers to more formally as 'Cluster Dissociation Disorder' (or CDD), is a disorder which causes individuals to dissociate or diverge from their cluster (notably, this is done voluntarily, which distinguishes it from cluster exile), leading to the standard effects of cluster isolation.


The commonly held idea in the scientific canon had been that CDD was caused by an otherwise unidentifiable infectious disease which, once established in the hosts brain, led to thought patterns which deviated from the cluster norm and caused undue stress on the cluster. This in turn led the individual to voluntarily isolate themselves for the protection of the cluster, for the greater good.


Dr. Margathe Lo and her team of researchers from AIO have put forward a radical and different idea: that CDD is not a disorder at all, but evidence of a differentiation within the cluster itself. The theory, which they claim is supported by macrogentic studies of orangutan clusters in the lab and in the wild, may have some startling implications which go against the very fundamental teachings of the field.

Supporters of the study claim that prior to the 'infection' theory gaining prominence, CDD was believed to be a form of 'biospiritual maleficence' which was brought on by the individual not fulfilling their duties to the cluster, which resulted in the 'divine punishment' of cluster isolation. They posit that the theory, while radical, isn't an unheard-of upheaval in our understanding of CDD, "just the next great leap of knowledge".


Critics of the study, on the otherhand, point to lack of homopoid studies and claim that the implications of the study grate against the well-proven theory that homonids were and are a clustering-dependent species. Head of Organometrics at the Union of Ameriga College (UOAC), Dr. Frank Armstead, held a press conference after the release of the study to answer questions from the press, in which he called the theory "a far-off fiction of credible creativity thought up by a deviant apologist [sic]". Dr. Lo, her team, and AIO refused to comment on this matter at this time.

Health and Living: Darklight in the Home

by: LaylaSaturn

Ever since the days of Becquerel and Roentgen's ground-breaking discovery, darklight has been a fascination of scientists within the relatively new field of lumenophysics. Yet, this fascination has failed to gain traction within other fields of the sciences. However, in this age of new discoveries and scientific advancement, individuals and research clusters from across the disciplines have taken interest in the phenomenon for its possible applications within their own fields of study.


Of keen interest to the editors of Epistemetry Monthly have been the recent offerings by the alchemists at DuPont S.A. who have over the past decade experimented with darklight's applications for consumer and industrial products. Last month DuPont's public relations team released a statement that they were "seeking to bring darklight into not just the Amerigan industrial sector, but the home of the avergage Amerigan consumer too", which would put alchemistry well ahead of other scientific disciplines in the race to take darklight out of the labs and into the hands of the culture at large.


DuPont and other large alchem manufacturers have made begun making overtures with large department store chains in the hopes of securing lucrative contracts for goods they plan to be in stores by the end of the year. DuPont for their part have started advertising their range of 'Luxon' branded cosmetics and cremes they claim will give a "fresh, vibrant look", as well as a line of mildly luminescent rouges. Imperial Tobacco Co. have teased a new line of cigarettes they suggest will contain darklight producing materials deisgned to "improve taste and reduce harshness", while Westinghouse are looking into the potential health benefits of darklight luminescence in home appliances such as washing machines and electric mops.


As government regulators attempt to assess the potential (if any) environmental and health effects of large scale production of darklight materials, it remains to be seen what new marvels science may bring to improve the standard of living for individuals and clusters across the union.
