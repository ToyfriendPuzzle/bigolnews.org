i am daphnis and i watch vtubers in my apartment whilst living my silly life.

if you dont know what a vtuber is, here's my definition, influenced by how my sister describes them: a performer's voice and sometimes body and eye movements are captured by a computer. 


these movements are translated into the animation of a character, almost like a puppet. their designs tend to be anime-inspired and often cute. this visual is streamed to an audience. 


they may play video games, hang out and conversate, or watch videos while reacting to chat.


you can find all of the fine vtubers i will discuss later in this piece on twitch dot com. that is, at least for now. it is true as of this writing that amaz*n continues to pay for the streaming website to exist. i am confident in saying that twitch does not make them much money.


i followed quite a few mainstream vtubers several months ago. i have now switched over to following primarily smaller vtubers. the focus of my viewing is on cozier streams, retro games, and women talking to me (clearly, i am the only audience member of note) about important subjects such as very specific roguelike mechanics.

i hesitate to use the word "cozy" due to the word's genrefication in gaming. what i mean by this descriptor is that i feel my heart beat slow down when i watch the stream and its leisurely pacing feels pleasing to me.


i like to be in an audience of 50 - 200 people. it is my experience that things get a little hectic as an audience member beyond that number. 


in a few paragraphs, i'll go over some of my favorite vtubers. their audiences range from small to large, and i've mostly left out the vtubers with the biggest audiences from my consideration and much of my viewing. please consider joining me in enjoying their works. most of the names listed here can be plugged directly into a twitch.tv/______
