WEIRD BUG FREED

By: SCRAMPLEPEANITS


A spectacular collaboration between the neighborhood Rats and Mice happened this past weekend! The two gangs worked together to lift up the cup from covering the Weird Bug In The Bathroom. Several insects and various bugs gathered to witness and celebrate together. "I just can't believe it, we never thought this would happen!" A ladybug shouted, tears in his eyes. Restaurant Shuts Down After Whole Staff Quits

PC: multifacetedgirl on Pixabay


A local restaurateur learned the impact of a bad hire the hard way when his entire staff quit in response to the new manager’s unique management approach. Jimmy Bimbini of Bimbini’s Bombastic Subs replaced the beloved manager of his decade-long establishment with known asshole Derek Bimbini, his ain’t shit nephew. Within days, the beloved restaurant changed from a hole-in-the-wall comfort food spot to a hole-in-the-ground shit food spot, thanks to Derek’s brilliant, cost-saving ideas like “lights off unless a customer walks in” and his strong “does mopping even do anything?” mentality.


Two days into the new management, the new cashier peed herself after being denied a bathroom break for 75 minutes because “breaks are for breaks” and she had taken her first 15 when she showed up three minutes late for her shift. At that point, pointed questions were asked of Jimmy Bimbini, like “has Derek worked in a restaurant before,” “isn’t he supposed to wash his hands after touching the dead rat in the basement,” and “can we kill this guy?” Met with no’s across the board, the staff initially went on strike. When the cops were called, they all collectively left their posts for good, with the long-time cook saying, “literally nothing is worth this.”


We reached out to Jimmy and Derek for comment, and all we received was a recording of a loud fart who the staff later identified as likely coming from Derek.

BEWARE! BLOODSUCKERS!

By: The Remote Viewer


was on a weed run in a luxurious black car with black interior, myself placed within a back seat which felt impossibly large, as the back seats of the 1959 edsel my father rebuilt after it was ruined by fire and left abandoned for decades did when i was small.


we, being me sitting in the car, and all the cars other occupants, who i have not yet mentioned, as i did not and still do not know any of them, arrived outside a brick building, and it was night, of course.


without any words spoken i knew that despite arriving at the correct location, we had not arrived into the correct situation. it was apparent to me, alone now, sitting in the impossibly large row of black back seats, that an attack upon the car and its inhabitants, who had at this point all left the car, other than me, was now under way.


i dove onto the soft black carpet of the floor and wedged myself against the back of the black passenger seat and shut my eyes until all i could see was black, minimizing the surface area of my body to potentially penetrating bullets, which never came.


i open my eyes to the driver's side back door being opened by who i immediately understood to be a vampire, a small man with black emo hair in a tight black suit. i pull my pocket knife out of my pocket, and interestingly it is not the pocket knife i've carried daily for the last 6 years, it's the first pocket knife i've ever owned. i rapidly unfold its stupid pliers so i can access the sub inch foldout knife on the handle that has never ever been sharpened and i wield the unwieldy; pliers pointing toward my chest, my impotent knife pointing toward my threat.


the monster man makes upon his round face an expression not remembered as he simply takes my pocket knife from me. okay. it is calmly explained to me that i must be a fucking idiot, because he doesn't kill his bloodbags, it really wouldn't make any sense to do that, where do i think the blood comes from? and anyway they're all going to his house for a party and i am freaking out.


i am in the emo vampire's dark home, sitting on a black couch, in an unlit room, with people who i still do not know, and around the room winds a black staircase to an upper floor with railings looking down upon this couch and the gigantic, powered-off television on the black stand in front of the black glass coffee table, sitting on the black rug. the stairs are a black metal, with a black carpet on each one, and you can see other dark corners of the dark room between their gaps. above hangs some kind of light fixture, also powered-off, and shrouded in shadow, as things that float in the middle of spaces tend to be. the upper floor has black carpets placed over some kind of floor that cannot be discerned in the complete darkness, similar to the furniture that is placed in likely unremarkable configuration.


out the front door is a city street. rows of townhomes line the road that bulges upon a hill. regular street lamps keep the place refreshingly illuminated in a perfect color-preserving white light. shrubbery and other decorative life glues road to sidewalk to lamp to yard. trees grow in the grass at intervals that would yield nice shade coverage in the sunny hours. the crickets chirp. cicadas cicade. the night sky blankets the scene with a coolness.


and so, i just leave.

Science and Nature

By: garbage_girl

PC: ulleo on Pixabay


Time began with Death. Before Death, Time had no meaning, no power, but when Death entered the world a pact was formed between them. Death would not come to Time, and Time became Death’s most reliable enforcer.


Before Time, there was water. The water was smaller than a needles head and without end. Many things swam in the water; deathless beasts who knew no ritual nor bond nor law, and whose shape and form would have blinded and confused any person if only there had been people around to witness them.


Now, when Time emerged she was first among smallfish, who were feed for the dogfish of her litter. Yet Time was not hunted by dogfish nor seen by her fellow smallfish, for no beast could see her, nor hear her, nor touch her. She was safe but loneliness was all she knew, and it was a void as great as the waters in which it swam, and she yearned for love.


All light in the void was the small light of lurking water and luminous smallfish. In one patch of water where lurking light always shone bright there was an isle of mud. This isle was barren. It had water both above and below it, for the sky was without weft; it had not yet been woven.


Upon the bank of mud came the Heron and the Osprey. Born from one egg, the Heron and Osprey were one bird, and when their egg cracked so entered Death.


With their eyes as one pair, Heron and Osprey saw deep into the water around them. They saw dogfish and deathless beasts and smallfish all alike. They hungered, but no fish called them as much as did Time. “Time,” the Bird said, their mouth one “We Hunger for you we Yearn for you” said the Heron and the Osprey. Time, whose loneliness could fill the largest gorge and deepest canyon, saw the Bird only with awe. “Be mine and I will give you power.” said the Heron. “Be with me and I will give you love.” said the Osprey. “I will take your power” said Time “I will take your love.”  The Bird opened their mouth and in swam Time. And so began the world.

death became him

By: perfect mind


i know i killed myself. i took my month’s medication in a day and i felt it hit my bloodstream. i was awake for 48 hours as the waves of pain hit me. i didn't know the words for serotonin syndrome until it hit me full force like a truck that kept revving its engine as it sat full weight on top of me.


but after i fell asleep, i woke up. and the world was ringing and loud, and i couldn't stand the way the lights seeped into the back of my eyes. i blinked and blinked but i couldn't remove the sensation of seeing, and feeling, from my groggy and itching lids.


you don't expect to wake up from forever.


i had hoped this time it would stick. all that stuck to me was the feeling of being watched and felt. a fly trap stuck full of flies,but there were more crawling on the walls. the scent of rotted fruit heavy in the air, which was already full of buzzing.


i was buzzing.


electrified.


i am, never was, afraid to die. and yet i’m still here. banging on the glass of my enclosure as insects take up more and more room beneath my skin.


i am, i was, a many-winged beast and i cannot stand the smell of old trash, leaking through my vents as i struggle to breathe. i am made of many parts and not one of them is whole on their own.


i am a hole and there are many things within me.


like the scent of death and the feeling of crawling under heavy lids.
