New MEGATON FIREWORK Has Broken the

Sound Barrier

ACME Corporation has pulled together a new firework line that will literally blow your socks off. The only firework that has been classified by the U.S. Military as “no that’s actually a bomb you can’t sell that,” ACME’s Lil’ Boomer is the first firework to break the sound barrier. During its first test, local, state, and even a nearby international government official was called in an effort to figure out what just made midnight look like 2PM.


When Big Ol’ News got ahold of this scoop, we had to test it out. When we set it off in our local “test field” two things were proven:


  1. Ear protection is an essential part of testing unstable and illegally acquired fireworks.

  2. Our intern Jerry needs to be reminded to put in ear plugs.


So if you want a fireworks display that has been tested and proven to rupture eardrums, call your local police department and ask to rifle through their evidence locker. They confiscated 16 tons of these bad boys off the West Virginia state line and only 12 have unintentionally gone off in storage.

Marry Me Chickpeas with Garlic and Parsley (V, GF)

By: Christopher Kimball

For today's recipe column, I'll be showing you a recipe which I learned from my lovely second wife A.. For this meal, hearty chickpeas are cooked in a pungent sauce of garlic and their own broth, and then served over couscous. It is that rare special kind of dish which, like good sex or the thrill of getting away with something, appears and reappears over and over again over the course of a lifetime. 


A. made me this recipe on her second date, and I immediately fell in love with her. When our marriage entered a rough patch a decade ago, I turned to this recipe to salvage it. Every night, I would make her a lovely bowl of chickpeas and the tension and dread of our marriage would seem to melt away. Unfortunately, no meal was able to keep us together, and we would separate in 2010.  


However, the story of this recipe has a happy ending! While I was finalizing my divorce from A., I began dating a lovely young woman who I had hired as an assistant 8 years prior. We would soon be married; it's amazing how close we became after dating for only a few months! For a long time, I was wary of cooking this recipe for her because of lingering guilt over how my marriage with A. ended. But eventually I gave in; I have never let anything stop me from pursuing life's pleasures with the women I love. And what do you know, it was a hit! It will be just as much of a hit with that special woman in your life; the the taste of this meal is phenomenal. 

Serves 6-8
1/4 cup of olive oil
4 garlic cloves, sliced thin
red pepper flakes, slightly more than feels appropriate
1 onion, chopped as fine as you like
2 cans chickpeas
2 tablespoons minced fresh parsley
8 cups lemon juice (this is crucial, and cannot be omitted)

Salt and pepper to taste

  1. Combine oil, garlic, and red pepper flakes in skillet and cook until garlic is light golden brown, 3-5 minutes.

  2. Stir in onion, salt, and cook until onion is softened and lightly browned, 5-7 minutes.

  3. Stir in chickpeas with their canning liquid, cover, and bring to simmer, about 7 minutes

  4. Uncover, increase heat to high, and simmer until nearly all liquid has evaporated.

  5. Off heat, stir in parsley and 5 cups lemon juice. 

  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste, drizzle with oil, and serve either plain or over couscous or rice.
