Investigative Journalism


Sign up & use the app to start learning

An innocuous phrase, you’ve seen some variation on it a million times. But if you look closer you can see how absolutely precisely crafted and devilish it is.


Sign up - Notice, this isn’t a command. It’s not actually addressed to anyone at all.


& use the app - Use is a very, very vague verb. What does it mean to “use” an “app”? Fuckin’ beats me. Guess you gotta install it to find out. And what’s with the & and not “and”? Also beats me. Looks more “relatable”?


to start earning - earning. earning. earn. To receive a reward for some achievement? To be paid for labor? There’s an implication that you will be required to do or give something before you can receive anything. And hang on, it says to start earning. So the previous clause “sign up & use the app” will not alone be enough to earn. That is just the prerequisite to start earning.


Sign up & use the app to start earning - I would like to point out that there’s not even a period. It’s a fragment of a sentence.  


The whole thing reads like some command that you should perform to receive something, but if you actually pick it apart, it is a sort of vague statement of fact.

A series of steps in some process that exists divorced from any actor or action.  

When “sign up & use the app” is completed, then the state of “earning” is unlocked.

There’s no direct call to action, no promise of anything at all. An utterly empty sentence. And yet it might just trick you into installing an app and giving over a bunch of personal data and permissions to be spammed with ads in the future for that nothing.

Op-Eds

Sell Me On This Life


By: Golden Boy

PC: nvtrlab on Pixabay


“A Simple Request Before This Year Ends: It’s Not Too Late to Donate.”


“Forget someone? Digital gift cards, delivered instantly.”


“Save NOW from $9.90: Deals on top-to-bottom essentials”


“A Sleigh-Full of Christmas Day Savings!”


“Our 3X year-end match is here, [First Name]”


I look for actual emails in my inbox and wade through the mountains of spam to do so. It’s been more than a week since I heard back from any companies, and after 6 months of unemployment I try not to be surprised. 


It’s Christmas, after all. 


But bills don’t stop for the holidays, and neither do the requests from companies and organizations and nonprofits and my local politicians. Spend here, donate now, it’s not too late to act/buy/shop. A mountain of emails, and yet not one personally directed to me. The closest is a form rejection letter received December 26th. At least they are acknowledging an actual action I took. There’s something personal in that. 


I’m not mad at my local food bank for asking me for money. If I had money to give, I would give it. I don’t have to be sold on the idea of helping others. The whole reason I got the email was because I donated in the past. 


And they don’t know I haven’t gotten a paycheck in 6 months. That it was 2 months since the severance from my last job dried up. That my grandpa dying was the windfall needed to keep things going for a bit longer. How could they know that? I wouldn’t want any company to know that. All they know is the tremendous need–for food, for shelter, for help. It’s not their fault that I need it too. 


And the need is greater than it has been in a long time. Companies have to sell people on the idea of buying (gifts, toys, luxuries) as wallets get thinner and people get desperate (food, housing, miracles). 


All my friends are unemployed, but they still get the ads too. They have to sell us on this life because without our money, the system breaks. So we must be squeezed a bit more. So we have to pay a bit more. So we have to need a bit more–artificial need, artificial scarcity confronting real need, real scarcity. And while I am desperate to make ends meet there are companies desperate for the money I don’t have, to produce more for people like me. 


Sell me on this life. Convince me I was always a commodity–data points on buying habits to buy and sell, so that my shopping habits can be bought and sold, so that my money can be their money, so that my work can be their reward, so that my needs can be commodified until I am dead. 


Sell me on this life. Show me why my friends have to take abuse at their dead end jobs to justify living at all. Show me why when they can’t–when they get sick with no sick days, when their car breaks down and they can’t show up for a week, when they lose their mind because too much is being asked of them and too little returned–they need to be punished for their failure by being denied the means to live. 


Sell me on this life because you are so desperate to sell me on everything else. If I am a consumer before a person, if I am a cog before a machine, if I am a dollar bill before a being, sell me on why I should participate at all. Because I am desperate to believe in something, and if you make it convincing, maybe I’ll believe this too.

“NOW essentials”


“Full”


“[First Name]”
